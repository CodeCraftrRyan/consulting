About

I came to data the long way — through museums, classrooms, and the operations of mission-driven organizations that needed to run better.

For more than a decade I've built the data and Salesforce systems that nonprofits and mission-driven teams quietly run on — currently as a director of data and technology at a national nonprofit, where I built the data function from the ground up. Before that, I spent years on the operations side of the sector: managing programs for an environmental education nonprofit, directing an education center and coordinating public programs at a major national museum, and serving in volunteer leadership for a local historical society. I once planned to be a museum director. The work I do now is, in many ways, the same work — making mission-driven organizations run better — just with different tools.

That long route shapes how I work. I know how nonprofit budgets, boards, volunteers, programs, and field staff actually behave — not in theory. I scope from lived experience, and I build things that fit the way these organizations really operate.
